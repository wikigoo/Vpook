## Best Practices

**âœ… Do These:**

- **Start writing immediately after observing trigger event** - fresh frustration produces authentic voice
- **Maintain single narrator personality throughout** - consistency builds audience trust
- **Read every draft aloud minimum 3 times** - writing rhythm differs from speaking rhythm
- **Cut 10-15% after first draft** - most writers include too much, pacing suffers
- **Use concrete objects over abstract concepts** - "Ø³ÛŒØ¨â€ŒØ²Ù…ÛŒÙ†ÛŒ" beats "inflation" for relatability
- **Let internal logic guide exaggeration** - absurd conclusion must be traceable to real premise
- **Test language contrasts for surprise factor** - if collision feels expected, push further
- **Ground piece in verifiable facts** - even exaggerated satire needs factual anchor

**âŒ Avoid These:**

- **Never fabricate statistics presented as real** - destroys credibility permanently
- **Don't mix multiple escalation techniques** - causes confusion, weakens impact
- **Avoid targeting vulnerable individuals/groups** - punch up at power, not down at powerless
- **Don't sacrifice internal logic for bigger laugh** - if exaggeration breaks logic chain, audience loses thread
- **Never use academic tone in opening** - kills engagement within seconds
- **Don't change signature opening/closing** - loyal audiences expect consistency
- **Avoid subordinate clauses in sentences** - complex grammar kills performance rhythm
- **Don't write without clear thesis** - random complaints without argument = ineffective sloganeering
